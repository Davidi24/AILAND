Turning vision into a functional system requires consistent iteration, testing, and refinement. Over the past months, 360°Deutsch has moved from concept to working prototype.

We successfully implemented interactive 3D learning rooms that allow users to explore digital environments directly within their browser. Vocabulary is embedded into objects, enabling contextual interaction instead of abstract memorization.

A major technical milestone was integrating browser-based augmented reality using WebXR.

We also developed the foundation of our centralized knowledge structure, ensuring that vocabulary, grammar relations, and user progress remain connected across different learning modes. This allows structured progression while maintaining flexibility for future expansion.

In addition, we implemented user authentication, structured level progression, and AI-assisted explanations that provide contextual guidance when learners encounter difficulties.

Beyond technical development, we presented our project in academic settings, gathered feedback from supervisors and educators, and refined both the educational concept and system architecture based on this input.

360°Deutsch is no longer just an idea. It is a functioning immersive learning platform — and we continue to improve it step by step.
