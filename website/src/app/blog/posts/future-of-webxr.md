Learning a language should not feel like solving disconnected puzzles. Vocabulary, grammar, listening, and speaking are not separate skills — they are parts of a single system.

With 360°Deutsch, we are building a platform that treats language as something interconnected and contextual.

Our goal is to create a browser-based learning experience where German is explored inside interactive 3D environments. Instead of memorizing isolated word lists, learners move through digital rooms and interact with objects in meaningful situations. Each object is connected to vocabulary, pronunciation, grammar, and example usage.

On mobile devices, augmented reality enhances the learning experience by placing interactive digital elements into the user’s physical environment. Instead of learning within a purely virtual space, learners can explore vocabulary through AR-based interaction, connecting language content to their surroundings in a more immersive way.

Behind the scenes, the platform is structured around a centralized knowledge system. Vocabulary and grammatical relationships are not stored as static entries but as connected elements within a semantic structure. This allows consistent progression, contextual learning, and intelligent adaptation.

We are not building just another language app. We are building an immersive learning environment designed to bridge the gap between memorization and real-world application.

360°Deutsch is our step toward contextual, experience-driven digital education.
